The **DMX View** shows your fixtures as a flowing list of panels, one per
fixture, each displaying its individual channels and their **live values**. It
is the best view for checking what every channel is currently outputting and for
setting a single channel by hand.

It is one of the four views available from the toolbar at the top of the
[Fixtures and Functions](FixturesAndFunctions.md) workspace. Use the **universe
selector** in that toolbar to limit the view to one universe.

## The fixture panels

Each fixture is drawn as a small panel listing its channels. For every channel
you can see its current DMX value (0–255), updated live as the output changes —
whether driven by your manual control, a running function, or an external input.

The panels flow to fill the available width and wrap onto new rows as needed.
Use the scrollbars (or the **zoom** buttons in the toolbar) if you have many
fixtures.

## Changing a channel value

Click a channel to open a control tool for it. Depending on the channel type
this is a slider or a preset chooser, letting you set that single channel's
value directly. The new value is sent to the output immediately.

This is a quick way to test an individual channel, or to nudge one value without
opening the full capability tools in the left panel.

## View settings

Click the **settings** button (the "bars" icon) in the toolbar to open the DMX
view's settings panel.

## Selecting fixtures

Selecting fixtures here works together with the rest of the workspace: a fixture
selected in the DMX View is also selected in the other views and in the left
panel's channel tools, so you can mix direct channel editing with the
higher-level capability controls.
